About YRSPP

Platforming early-career researchers since 2018

Our goals

The primary aim of YRSPP is to facilitate the exchange of cutting-edge research in plant photobiology among ECRs on a low-pressure international stage. The spotlight will be on them. ECRs will deliver talks and present posters of their work, allowing them to showcase their findings and receive constructive feedback from peers and more senior researchers. YRSPP's central focus on ECR participation underscores our commitment to nurturing the careers of young and upcoming investigators in the field.

Our speakers

As well as a host of presentations by our attending ECRs, four keynote speeches by newly appointed assistant professors will help inspire the next steps of our attendees' careers and allow them to build valuable research connections throughout the event. YRSPP 2026 will also feature two career-focussed presentations by established photobiologists - this year Professor Ute Höcker and Professor Kerry Franklin - to provide insights into a range of successful academic trajectories.

Societal and scientific impact

Plant photobiology is of international importance as it intersects with critical global challenges like climate change, food security, sustainable agriculture and renewable energy. Understanding how plants respond to light - and how these responses affect their growth, development, and adaptation to changing environmental conditions - will underpin crucial innovations in crop productivity, ecosystem resilience and new biotechnology.

The research that will be discussed at YRSPP events has the potential to drive these innovations by fostering collaboration and knowledge exchange among ECRs, helping to propel the international community towards groundbreaking discoveries and sustainable solutions to pressing global challenges. By engaging senior researchers and assistant professors, YRSPP reinforces its commitment to mentorship and knowledge transfer, equipping early-career investigators with the connections and skills to make a lasting impact on plant photobiology research on a global scale.

Our history (and future!)

The Young Researchers Symposium on Plant Photobiology (YRSPP) originated in early 2018 in a conference centre elevator in Matsue, Japan, where frustrated PhD students discussed the limited involvement of young scientists in the significant International Symposium on Plant Photobiology (ISPP). To address this gap, the first YRSPP was organised in Cologne later that year and facilitated successful networking between more than 50 young researchers.

Subsequently, a team formed over drinks decided to organise a second YRSPP for 2020, which had to be postponed to 2022 due to the pandemic. The next team organised another highly successful event in Utrecht in 2024, continuing to create a supportive environment to connect and collaborate with the goal of advancing photobiology research. A new team is now organising YRSPP 2026 in the same spirit, and we're looking forward to welcoming the next cohort of ECRs in Edinburgh!
